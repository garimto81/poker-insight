Why Online Poker Traffic is Important

When it comes to online poker sites, traffic really matters for quite a few reasons. For starters, it gives you a sense of how popular a site or network is. That popularity usually translates to more cash games and tournaments happening throughout the week. And yes, you guessed it, that means more chances for you to play.

It’s also a helpful way to spot the safer, more reliable sites, especially if you’re learning how to play poker and aren’t sure where to start. That said, just because a poker room is big doesn’t automatically mean it’s the right fit for youmore on that later.

How to Read PokerScout’s Online Traffic Report

We’ve made our report as simple as possible so you can compare the best online poker sites at a glance. But before you make any decisions, here’s a quick rundown of the terms we use.

  • Players Online – Number of players currently playing online at that site or network.
  • Cash Players – Number of players currently playing poker online for real money at ring game tables.
  • 24 H Peak – The highest number of active players in the last 24 hours.
  • 7 Day avg – The average number of active players over the past seven days.
  • Last Week – Peak number of active players each day over the past week.

You can also use the ‘Select a Location’ tab to choose networks in your current location or worldwide.

How Can I Trust PokerScout’s Traffic Numbers?

All data in our traffic report is based on player numbers taken from the lobby at online poker rooms or provided directly to us by the operator.

While PokerScout is a site built on trust and reputation, we don’t expect you to take everything you read as fact. You can easily check the numbers in question by downloading a site’s software, checking the number of players in the lobby, and comparing it to our report of the top online poker sites.

Why Are the Player Numbers So Low in Comparison to the Poker Sites Lobby?

The best online poker sites for real money often display the number of connected players or online players in their lobbies. Its not always clear what this number represents, but the sites often use the broadest possible definition of a player.

This definition includes:

  • Play-money players
  • Freeroll tournament players
  • Real-money tournament players
  • Cash game players
  • Players who are logged in but not playing

At PokerScout, we track and rank sites based on cash game players, which is a much smaller (but more relevant) number. Where possible, we also show the number of online players for comparison. But, because this number is undefined, we don’t fully rely on it.

Real Money Deposit and Withdrawal Methods at Online Poker Sites

To play online poker for real money, you’ll have to deposit funds. After winning, you’ll be able to withdraw from the account just as easily. The top poker sites make this process very simple and generally provide a range of banking methods to choose from.

Specific payment methods will depend on the poker site in question, but these are the main options:

Credit and Debit Cards

Credit and debit cards are the most popular payment methods for online transactions. They’re available worldwide, and most players are already familiar with them. Visa and Mastercard are the two biggest brands here, along with Discover, American Express, and others.

Cards allow for fast deposits and are accepted on most online poker websites. Since players already have at least one card readily available, payments are quick and to the point. You just have to enter your card details on the site and then approve the transaction.

But not all poker sites online will support withdrawals to cards, so you may need to use another method to cash out.

Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrency banking is still fairly new when it comes to online poker on a global scale. More and more sites accept cryptos for deposits and also allow players to withdraw back to their wallets, without requiring users to verify their identity.

The downside is that they’re not as popular as other banking methods, and new players will have to set up their wallets and buy coins in advance. Modern Bitcoin poker sites aim to make deposits and withdrawals as simple as possible, even for beginners. They also accept several coins and process transactions instantly.

Bank Transfers

Online poker sites with real money banking support can accept direct transfers for deposits and withdrawals. This way, you can send money directly from your bank account to the site and then back again after cashing in some pots.

Since this is done mainly through mobile apps these days, bank transfers can be quite efficient.

You should note that this banking method is not the fastest for online transactions. Some bank transfers require additional verifications that can take up to five business days, depending on your bank. Transaction fees can also be quite large, sometimes going up to $50.

Other Popular Banking Methods for Online Poker Sites

Additional deposit options can include eWallets, such as PayPal, Skrill, and Neteller. These web wallets were designed for quick transactions online, so they can be convenient once you set everything up. But, due to local regulations and refund policies, most poker sites have moved away from eWallets.

Mobile payment services, like Apple Pay and Google Pay, are similar to eWallets but tend to be more available. These allow you to connect a card to your web wallet and then make transactions from your phone. You can also use mobile payment services to fund other banking methods.

The list goes on to include prepaid vouchers, which you can purchase online or at retail stores before then depositing on the site.

Some poker sites will even accept cash payments if they have a partnership with a land-based casino in your area. You deposit at a retail casino, which will then appear in your online account.
